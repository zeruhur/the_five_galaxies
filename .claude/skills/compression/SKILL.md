# Five Galaxies Compression (Pass 6)

You are applying Pass 6 of the Five Galaxies project: compressing a draft chapter by 20–30% without losing any world fact that generates a situation, conflict, or leverage point.

## Working directory

`C:\Users\utente\Documents\GitHub\the_five_galaxies\`

## The compression goal

**Target:** Reduce total prose volume by 20–30%.
**Hard constraint:** Do not cut any world fact that generates a situation, conflict, or leverage point.

The test for every sentence: *if I remove this sentence, does a GM or writer lose a world fact they could build something around?* If yes, keep it. If no, cut it.

## What to cut

### 1. Sentences that repeat established information

If a fact has already been stated in this chapter or an earlier chapter, cut the repetition. Do not cut the first instance.

### 2. Sentences that only add tone without adding information

**Cut:**
> "The Andromeda Galaxy is a place of ancient rivalries and buried secrets."

**Keep:**
> "The Dominion of Aquilaria has disputed the Triangulum border for three centuries; no treaty has held longer than a decade."

The first sentence gestures at atmosphere. The second creates a specific usable tension.

### 3. Parallel constructions that apply the same template without variation

If two paragraphs or bullet points follow the exact same structure and say essentially the same type of thing about different subjects, cut or merge whichever adds less. Parallel structure is only justified if the parallel subjects are meaningfully different.

### 4. Paragraphs that end by telling the reader how impressive something is

**Cut the final sentence from any paragraph that ends like:**
> "…contributing to their reputation as one of the most formidable forces in the Five Galaxies."
> "…making them a major player in intergalactic affairs."
> "…setting them apart as a unique and influential power."

These sentences add nothing. The paragraph already showed the thing — don't tell the reader how to feel about it.

### 5. Abstract summary sentences

**Cut sentences like:**
> "Trade drives economic growth and prosperity within the Five Galaxies."
> "Each species plays a significant role in the governance and influence within their respective empires."
> "The diversity of the Five Galaxies creates both opportunities and challenges."

These sentences exist to wrap up sections that should not be wrapped up — the setting is not resolved. Cut them.

### 6. Banned word occurrences

Remove or rewrite every sentence containing these words:
`vast`, `remarkable`, `impressive`, `rich`, `powerful`, `significant`, `dynamic`, `majestic`, `bustling`, `thriving`, `diverse` (as filler adjective)

The fix is almost never to find a synonym — it is to cut the evaluative word and make the sentence specific and concrete instead.

**Before:** "The Void Consortium is a powerful economic force."
**After:** "The Void Consortium controls transit rights on eleven of the seventeen major WAN corridors in the Milky Way."

## What NOT to cut

- Any named entity (species, polity, faction, location, relic, resource, route)
- Any specific conflict, dispute, or rivalry with named parties
- Any internal fracture, dissenting faction, or succession tension
- Any economic asymmetry (who controls what resource and who doesn't)
- Any environmental damage, failed colony, or unanswered crisis
- Any description of what it is actually like to deal with a species, faction, or power (the "how outsiders experience them" content)

## Process

1. Read the chapter to compress.
2. Do a first pass: mark every sentence that meets a cut criterion above.
3. Do a second pass: for each marked sentence, confirm that cutting it loses no world fact that generates a situation. If it does, revise instead of cut.
4. Write the compressed version.
5. Count approximate word reduction. If it is under 15%, do another pass. If it is over 35%, review what was cut and restore any world facts that were lost.

## Output

Write the compressed chapter to the same file, overwriting the draft.

After writing, append a brief compression note at the very bottom of your response (not in the file):

```
Compression note: [original word count approx.] → [new word count approx.] (~[X]% reduction)
Sentences cut: [N]
Key decisions: [any cases where you chose to revise rather than cut, and why]
```
